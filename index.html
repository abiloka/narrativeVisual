1. Timeline of the world labor trends

This scene shows the trends of world's labor participation rates, ratio of female to male labor participation, and unemployment rates over the period from 2000 to 2020.
  • The labor participation has been relatively steady around 60% on average around the world.
  • Labor force participation ratio of women to men decreased slightly by 2020 dipping below 50%.
  • Decrease in gender ratio of labor participation is negatively correlated with an increase in unemployment rates in 2020.

    • A detailed description of rates is available at: https://datatopics.worldbank.org/world-development-indicators/themes/people.html

    Orange line is for labor participation rate, green is the ratio of female to male labor force participation rate, and red is for unemployment rate

    2. Labor participation in the U.S.

    This scene presents a plot of labor trends in the United States from 2020 to 2020.
  • In the U.S., both labor participation and gender ratio rates decreased slightly over the last 20 years.
  • Unemployment rate in the U.S., although coincided with the decrease in labor participation, did not correlate with the ratio of female to male labor participation.

    • A detailed description of rates is available at: https://datatopics.worldbank.org/world-development-indicators/themes/people.html
